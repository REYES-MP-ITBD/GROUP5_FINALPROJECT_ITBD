Content Marketing



How to create a content marketing strategy



           In the very least, no two content marketing tactics can be the same. In the most basic level, "blogging" and "video marketing" are both strategies. However, looking deeper into the tactics, schedules, and goals of those techniques shows a great deal of variability and complexity.


  1. Select promotions and distribution channels your target audience uses.

Do they use unique social media sites to gather information, or do they rely on email or search engines?


  1. Establish how the marketing team can help deliver on those goals.

Create content for the middle of the funnel that will help prospects move closer to a conversion.


  1. Understand which buyer personas youâ€™re targeting with your content.

Who in your target market has the financial means to become a paying customer?


  1. Decide which types of content are preferred by those buyers.

Is this persona more interested in short posts or video demos and face-to-face meetings?


  1. Map conversion pathways to the customer journey.

At which point in the funnel do you want to boost conversions and eliminate any last-minute consumer roadblocks?


  1. Design calls-to-action and event triggers for tracking and measuring performance and goal completions.

           Create clickable CTAs that lead to dedicated landing pages and monitor those clicks, connections, and behavior with code and integration into your analytics dashboard to better attribute performance and identify drop-off.


  1. Create a template, model or workflow that organizes and facilitates the execution of the above steps.

To coordinate the campaign, use agile technique, project management tools, creative agency suppliers, or other "systems."


  1. Identify short- and long-term business goals.

40% increase in customer subscriptions in every quarter.








Content marketing in the sales funnel




            The sales funnel is the fundamental building block of marketing and sales coordination. Knowing how your funnel is built, how each stage works, and where changes can be made empowers you to build content that will resonate and convert with any company.



There are four constituent parts: Awareness, Interest, Consideration, and Purchase

Awareness

             Users are just learning about your brand and aren't waiting to be sold to or convinced.


types of content are best-suited

  • Blog Articles
  • Social Media Posts
  • Infographics
  • Podcasts
  • Short Video

    • Consideration

                 Users are more intent-driven than ever before, and they're constantly comparing your brand to others to see whether your products or services meet their specific needs.


    types of content are best-suited

  • Case studies
  • Customer testimonials
  • Web demos
  • FAQs
  • Sales sheets

    • Interest

                 Users may be fascinated by your offers and want to learn more about your company or follow you on social media.


    types of content are best-suited

  • White papers
  • eBooks
  • Explainer videos
  • Conversion landing pages
  • Display ads

    • Purchase

                 After thoroughly vetting your brand, users are happy to become paying customers. All that's needed is a swipe of a card or the signature of a contract./p>

    types of content are best-suited

  • Sales emails
  • On-site video testimonials
  • Sample or trial content

    •              As your team completes the above assets, you'll be able to precisely deploy them at the appropriate stage of the funnel. Both the marketing and sales teams can function in full harmony, ideally with the help of sales enablement tools, so you don't mix your messaging or confuse prospects with uncontextualized material.
