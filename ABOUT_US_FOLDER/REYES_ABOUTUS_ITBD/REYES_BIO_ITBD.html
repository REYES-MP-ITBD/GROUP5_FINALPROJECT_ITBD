Childhood Years

My life began when I was at the age of 4.1 I do have a lot of memories that I will never forget.2 I was this jolly, energetic, and optimistic kid who loves to wrap her arms around anyone, especially to my dad.3 I was a friendly kid who loves to be friends with anyone.4 I used to play a lot with my brother and that means I do love to play boys' games.5 I wasn't a fan of girly things since I was a kid but I do have a lot of girlfriends at that time.6 Running and hiding from someone is my thing.7 I was really adventurous and willing to try new things even though it scares me.8 Just like other kids, I do have weaknesses that I don't have any idea how to cope with during those times.10 My biggest fear was being alone.11 Not literally, but having no one by my side during my darkest days.12 At the age of 7, I've experienced one of the biggest challenges in my life.13 My parents were separating and I had no idea of their reasons.14 I was having my siesta at my grandmother's room when I heard two people shouting.15 They were fighting over me regarding where am I gonna stay, if it's with my daddy or my mommy.16 They even pulled me in different directions and I ended up being with my mom.17 I will never forget this memory because I witnessed this kind of my parents' argument.18 When I was 10, my parents came up with the idea of staying with my dad during weekends and weekdays with my mom.19 That only happened one summer because they've decided to let us stay with my dad for us to go easier in school.20 After those confusing setups, I can't help but not to question my self.21 Questions were running in my head if my brother and I were not enough for them to stay together.22 I know they have their reasons but still, it did hurt a lot.23 Just then I realized that there's nothing I can do but to accept everything and move on.24 I kept reminding myself that I will not let this pain consume me, make me do stupid things, and waste my life.25 I even pursue harder to prove anyone belittling me that even though I came from a broken family, that won't stop me from achieving my goals and make my parents proud.26

Teenage Years

Years past and adjustments were made, I reached the most exciting stage of my life; being a teenager.1 My badminton career started in this stage though my interest in playing badminton started when I was 6 years old.2 My dad and I used to play badminton a lot but then he was assigned to Davao and that was a huge adjustment for me.3 I was in Grade 7 having huge adjustments with the environment and the curriculum of that school while he was far from me.4 At that time, it was hard for me to adjust because I'm not the same friendly little girl as I used to be.5 I've undergone a major change in my attitude.6 I became the total opposite of my self when I was young.7 As I adapt to the environment, I met some friends who became my companion and made me tough enough during those days.8 Alongside, I pursued my father's dream to continue his legacy in playing sports.9 Since my brother doesn't play any sport, I was the one who made that dream come true for my dad.10 I became a student-athlete since I was in Grade 7 though I wasn't competing at that time.11 I started competing when I was in Grade 8.12 I was very nervous but then my dad was always there for me to support and guide me during my games.13 He was my personal coach and my number one supporter.14 He motivated me to train harder and achieve my goals in my badminton career.15 I was able to compete in the regionals and didn't win a medal but gave me a lot of lessons and pushed me harder to train more.16 I competed in regionals for 3 years straight but then I got a back injury.17 That injury made me question myself and decided not to pursue my dream to compete in UAAP and just settle in Cavite because I struggled in that back injury when I was training.18 I planned my life to give up my badminton career in college and just focus on my studies.19 Before my graduation, someone messaged me to try out at the University of the East.20 At first, I was hesitating but then my dad and I had a deep conversation.21 That made me realized that there's nothing I would lose so I did try out.22 That decision made all of my plans changed.23

College Years

I always fear the thought of being a college student.1 It was the stage where we were going to make our path to the career we want to pursue.2 I was really confused about which course should I take but one thing for sure is that I will never continue my sports career.3 The only thing that changed my mind was the time when I went to the try-out at the University of the East.4 Before entering any university, I went to some try-outs like in National University in Manila and De La Salle University in Cavite.5 I was thinking of trying at Adamson University but I was too late to their scheduled try-outs.6 I talked to my dad so I can decide which university should I choose and ended up entering UE.7 I had the best hardworking and supportive teammates.8 We had a great time before the semester started.9 We really had fun at our training.10 As the semester started, my teammates were the ones who pushed and motivated me to go out of my comfort zone.11 They reminded me always that I shouldn't be shy to meet new people.12 My skills in determining who are the people I should trust were tested.13 Manila is one of the dangerous place but it will teach you a lot of important life lessons.14 placesMonths passed, the UAAP season started and we had to double our hard work in training.15 We need to train more because we had this goal to at least never let our school rank last.16 I was really nervous at that time because of my injury, the pressure, and the thought of playing in front of so many people.17 It was a hard time for me because even though I competed in a lot of schools when I was in high school it still feels different to play in a high-level competition like UAAP.18 My family, high school best friends, and some of my college friends supported me during my games.19 I received a lot of messages from them, handwritten letters to boost my confidence, and one of them watched.20 Those little things really made me confident enough to try my very best in all my games.21 We weren't able to achieve our goal because most of us were just rookies but we had a lot of realizations and lessons.22 I was touched when my dad told me that he was so proud of me during my games.23 The thought of me playing in UAAP made him really happy.24 I became more motivated to improve my skills after hearing those stuff.25 I don't want to waste all the time and effort my dad had given me.26 All the personal training we had, traveled far to join tournaments, and all the deep talks we had to boost my morale in playing.27 All my achievements are dedicated to my parents especially to my dad who sacrificed a lot for me to reach where I am right now.28
