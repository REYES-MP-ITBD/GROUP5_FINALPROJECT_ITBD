Mifuneyama Rakuen

4100 Takeochō Ōaza Takeo, Takeo-shi, Saga-ken 843-0022, Japan


Discussion:

Mifuneyama Rakuen is a park that was established by Nabeshima Shigeyoshi the 28th ruler of the Takeo Domain. The park covers 150,000 square meters (about 10 Tokyo Domes) and took three years to complete. Mifuneyama Rakuen was to be made part of Nabeshima Shigeyoshi’s holiday home when it was finished in 1845. It was styled to be a stroll garden in order to enjoy the artistic arrangement of foliage. This wonderful garden can be found in Takeo city in Saga Prefecture at the western foot of Mount Mifune.

How to get there:

The nearest train station is JR Takeo-Onsen Station, from where it’s a 30 minute walk. You can also opt to take a bus from JR Takeo-Onsen headed for Ureshino Onsen and alight at Mifuneyama Rakuen.
