Content Marketing



Content marketing distribution strategy



               Getting the right distribution networks in place isn't always enough to ensure that the content reaches its full potential. You must also optimize certain particular channels to ensure that they are being used to their full potential.If you want to maximize email marketing conversions but don't have email lists segmented efficiently by audience variations, you may be reaching too many people at once â€“ and converting no one.



  1. Fix all indexing issues and mark up your site with proper schema.

Search crawlers must be able to correctly interpret the site and deliver it to users in SERPs for organic search to act as a distribution channel.


  1. Solicit micro influencer contributions.

Your marketing allies are experts in your field who share your content, and they will help you take your content to new heights you couldn't have reached on your own. Request that they add a quote to your next post, enter your podcast, or work on joint projects with you.


  1. Podcasting

Podcast marketing is gaining traction as audiences who were once classified as "readers" or "viewers" become "listeners." In recent years, the number of people who consume audio content has doubled, and many who listen to podcasts on a weekly basis do so for more than 6 hours. You can't miss such a large audience.


  1. Maximize email signature real estate.

Why not make your email signature more actionable if you're already sending emails? Include contact information, call-to-action buttons, product catalogs, and links to recent material, among other things.


  1. Webinar marketing

A webinar can be made out of almost any form of material. The narrated, slideshow, Q&A format boosts viewer interaction, generates leads through registrations, and makes your content more versatile.


  1. Guest blogging for backlinks.

Posting on reputable, high-DA sites will send additional referral traffic your way and, over time, raise your own site's DA, which is a top Google ranking factor.


  1. Use browser notifications where applicable.

With the click of a button, you can send targeted messages to users on your site, and if they agree, you've added another person to your email list. It only takes a few minutes to create browser alerts, and you can produce a lot of activity in a short amount of time. Only be wary of the accuracy of the clicks you get.


  1. Measured paid distribution.

Without proper monitoring and targeting, pay-per-click ads on search engines and social media sites can be expensive. When done correctly, however, you can hit a larger number of users in a shorter amount of time than depending solely on organic marketing. Using paid media delivery as a smaller percentage of your budget and be strategic with your investments.


  1. Segment your email lists and set up custom marketing triggers.

Email marketing can get quite complicated very quickly, but it's the ideal channel for keeping track of virtually all that happens at all times. Track every event, cause, or activity as thoroughly as possible, and then feed that information back into your next email campaign.


  1. Ideate distribution channels at the same time you brainstorm content ideas.

As a result, every asset is perfectly matched and on target with your goals.
