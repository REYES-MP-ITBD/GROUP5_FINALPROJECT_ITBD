THE DIFFERENCE OF ORGANIC AND INORGANIC MARKETING

In Digital Marketing, you often deal with these terms called organic and inorganic digital marketing. This article will help you understand and define the difference between organic and inorganic digital marketing.

Organic Digital Marketing refers to techniques in gathering customers without paying any charges for advertisements on any platform. It is simply free marketing strategies. Organic Marketing refers to the act of getting your customers to come to your digital channel naturally over time, rather than artificially paid links or boosted posts. It doesn't require monetary value, but you have to invest your time and effort if you are going to pursue this technique. Content and consistency play a major role here. The campaigns here maintain the interest of current followers and build an online community.

Even if it is free, it doesn't mean that it is easy. Growing an online presence can be quite hard and tricky nowadays. You have to gain the attention and the trust of your potential customers. Using strategic ways like hashtags, stories, live feed, direct messages, and even word-of-mouth to increase customers' interest. Reviews and recommendations from your followers are examples of word-of-mouth marketing that makes it the most effective way to increase online presence in Organic Marketing. Being strategic with your posts and updates about your business might lead to much more authenticity to your followers and your business as well. You can slowly build your online community for every trustworthy transaction between your customers. With this, you can have a much wider reach to your audience.

Factors in Organic Marketing Strategies:

  • Content - advertisements should have good content to capture more attention of the audience. Engaging content will be a really big help to your advertisement.
  • Search Engine Optimization - optimize your websites for search engine visibility. It would ruin your efforts if you have good content but with a bad SEO approach.
  • Social Media - choosing the right social media platforms to reach more audiences. Also, creating business social profiles to assure your customer that you are easy to communicate with.

On the other hand, Inorganic Marketing is the opposite of Organic Marketing. It involves paid advertisements like banner ads and sponsored posts to showcase your ads to the users as they browse through the digital platforms. Unlike Organic Marketing, Inorganic Marketing helps you reach a specific group of people you like to reach. Examples of paid advertisements are Google Ads, Facebook Ads, and LinkedIn Ads. The good thing about Inorganic Marketing is that whenever your product/service is offered for a certain period you can immediately boost your advertisements.

It still depends on your target audience what type of marketing you are going to pursue for your business. You can either choose organic wherein it is free yet quite tricky to make a name in the online world, or you can go with inorganic wherein paid banner ads and sponsored posts. Choose wisely on what you think will benefit most your business.
