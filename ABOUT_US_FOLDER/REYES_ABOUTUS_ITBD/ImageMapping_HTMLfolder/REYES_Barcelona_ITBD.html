BARCELONA

Sagrada Familia, the unfinished church and the number 1 attraction in Barcelona. The construction of the holy cathedral started on March 19, 1882, and was initially based on the plans of architect Francisco de Paula del Villar. Suddenly in 1883, it was given to architect Antoni Gaudi to continue the construction. He was given an anonymous donation and so he decided to replace the original Gothic Revival design with a more modern and innovative temple design. The construction was halted for a few years during the Spanish Civil war until he died in 1926. The unfinished cathedral will presumably be completed in 2026.



After arriving at the Barcelona El Prat Airport and wanted to go directly to Sagrada Familia, you can ride an Aerobus that will take you to Plaza Catalunya. It is the heart of Barcelona, therefore you have options in going to the church. You can either take a bus or a metro. Another good place to visit here in Barcelona is the Picasso Museum. The highlights of this museum are the two biggest works of Pablo Picasso, the First Communion (1896) and Science and Charity (1897). The Picasso Museum reveals Picasso's connection to the city of Barcelona indicating the shaping of his youth, adolescence, up until his death.
