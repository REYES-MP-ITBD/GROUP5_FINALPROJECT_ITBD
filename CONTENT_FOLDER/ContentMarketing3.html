Content Marketing



How to generate content ideas



Building Ideas

           There’s no shortage of tools, tricks and tips for generating content ideas. If the primary roadblock to your marketing is volume generating ideas in bulk could be worthwhile. Conversely, if your content continually misses the mark due to poor strategy, your goal may be to produce ideas that are more targeted and valuable to a smaller subset of high-conversion readers.

  1. Social media

           Following trending news, viral events, and high-engagement threads keeps you informed of what's going on in your field and where you may be able to contribute.


  • Twitter Trends for you

    • The first is "Trends for you," which are high-level occurrences focused on who you follow and the types of tweets you communicate with most often. And there's "What's going on," which refers to broad national or pop-culture issues.

  • Reviews/Feebacks of the customer

    • Google My Company, the Better Business Bureau, and other online directories will provide a wealth of ideas. You may also ask for customer feedback by filling out a questionnaire on your website or at the end of a phone call. Regardless, customer reviews are very straightforward and actionable in the sense that you can build content right away based on what they say.

  • Hashtags on different soc-med platform

    • This is a simple one. Using hashtags, you can easily find topics to discuss on any social network or through a social automation app. A simple hashtag like #ContentMarketing will launch you into a world of trending, relevant topics.

  • BuzzSumo

    • What is being posted on social media in reality? Is it true that some material performs well on some channels but not others? Is there any timely material that needs to be published right now?

  • In demand on the market

    • What are the things that the customer demand on the market? The trending product on the market made by the demands of the customer.


    1. In-house

               Your company's workforce is likely to have decades of business and technological expertise and they're only a few desks away. Inquire with various departments, brainstorm with sales teams, and learn more about the subject matter at hand by looking inward to those employees..


  • Buyer Persona

    • Creating buyer personas is a standard marketing strategy. Then, no matter how static or shapeless such personas seem, marketers attempt to wrap content around them.

  • Keyword target on trend

    • Simply enter a target keyword or expression, and the tool will produce multiple lists of potential ideas based on actual search results.

  • Stage in the sales funnel

    • Clients also request new leads, but only via blog material. That's not how it works. So many marketing campaigns concentrate on specific stages of the funnel. The topics should be intent-driven, and the content should be funnel-specific.

  • Collaborate with Sales
  • Mine proprietary research

    • Marketers consider proprietary research to be their most effective link-building strategy. We, too, are huge fans of it. You're inherently at the forefront of a given area of interest if you're the only real arbiter and publisher of applicable data insights. You also don't need to be a data scientist.


    1. On-SERP options

               Google has a variety of useful, simple-to-use features right in the SERPs that will help you figure out what to write about.


  • Trends on social media

    • They not only tell you what's "in," but also what's really fascinating or buzzworthy. Using previous social media success to generate new ideas and increase feedback on subsequent posts can benefit.

  • Recommendations

    • Autosuggestions are perhaps the most convenient method for compiling this list since they complete your searches for you. By doing so, Google automatically offers a dropdown of suggestions for your question, all of which are based on real search patterns.

  • Related content in searched keyword

    • Enter a keyword or phrase in Google, then scroll to the bottom of Page 1 to see the results. You'll see "Searches linked to" and a few other keywords and phrases there. There are real Google searches that give you insight into what people are thinking.

  • People's questions

    •  Google now displays “People even ask” at the top of the SERPs (typically above the fold). This snippet displays additional questions that people have asked about the keyword you typed in. The link “People also ask” is clickable, and the further you go, the more choices you'll see. Create content based on the answers to these questions.

    1. SEO tools

               Understanding search queries can be improved through delving deeper into keyword-driven concepts. Then, working backward, you can build content the fulfills that search purpose.

  • Google Analytics

    • GA is a gold mine of useful information about your content's results. Which of your current pages is attracting the most visitors? What is the quality of this traffic? What keywords did you use? You have complete control of how the content is broken up, segmented, and analyzed, which can provide you with a plethora of new content ideas in the future. Discover what works and do more of it.

  • Answer the public

    • Answer the Public is a master at visualizing concepts in a variety of formats. They link ideas together using keywords, questions, prepositions, alphabetical order, and other methods. It's totally open, and all you have to do is scrape the most relevant key phrases for your target market.

  • MarketMuse

    • MarketMuse, a paid tool that creates personalized content reports that display you additional keywords and subtopics you might be writing about, is another great tool. It also informs you how many words you'll need to write to outrank other pages and cover the subject as extensively as possible. In the future, each associated subtopic may be treated as a separate post.
